Shum white noise player

This was the first project for a real client that I ever worked on as a UX/UI designer. It was a 48h hackathon project which ended up as the most innovative solution and winning project for a white noise player by Brainster.co

Team members : Mayda Maksut, Mila Mitrovska, Simona Gjosheva & Filip Dimkov

Task

Inspired by the world's covid pandemic, the stakeholders got an idea to create a product that will help the people who work from home usually alone. The task was to create easy to use white noise player , a player that the users will mainly use while working or studying. A player that you open to choose and play a sound and leave it like that playing in the background for the whole working/studying time.

This was a team project and it was first for all of the members of the team so we all contribute to most of its stages. Besides this, I can say that in the final stage of the design I mainly focused on the prototyping.

white noise
Player
Approach
Research

In the market research, we have researched several direct and indirect competitors. We found out that most white noise players do not put much effort into the visual appearance of their applications, the main accent of this type of player is on the functionality and KISS (keep it simple, stupid) designing principle.


Because this is a simple app without many options and features, we discovered that the most important main thing that we need to do is to represent the brand identity of the client and incorporate the needed features.

Users



The target users for this project were pre-defined by the stakeholders. Their age range was between 21-35 years old, our users were people that work from home for some time and try to improve their performance and make their working environment as productive as possible.

Having these characteristics in mind we as a team manage to interview more than 10 people in our target group and learned a few interesting stuff about their perspective on this kind of apps and their needs. The persona that we designed for was Dorian.



Dorian


Dorian is a 26-year-old programmer who has been working from home for a year due to the pandemic. He likes to hike and listen to music and misses the working atmosphere from the office. Dorian is a person that believes in constant improvement and likes to try new things.

Two things that we discovered during the interviews that weren't mentioned in the client's brief were that the users would like to have an option to automatically mix sounds once they open the app and use a timer for stopping the sound after chosen period of time.



Wireframes and sketches

After we gathered all of the important information from the research and set our ideas and goals for the project we started wireframing.
The main principles that we went for were:
- minimal design
- easy to use
- based on symbols
